this is how tahitian pearls are harvested komoka pearl started farming pearls over 30 years ago its process is different because it keeps the oysters alive for several generations tahitian pearls come in a variety of colors the oysters are brought to the operating table for the gentle removal of the pearl a clamp holds the oyster in place and pliers hold the oyster open the oyster is open the smallest amount possible this is done to avoid injuring the muscle that keeps the shell closed the pearl is removed using tools that look like dental instruments they make an incision for the pearl blank or nucleus and the piece of mantle tissue the mantle tissue has been taken from another oyster this is what will decide the color of the future pearl and ensure that the nucleus has pearl naked deposited in it once this is done the oyster goes back into the water it's put inside a protective basket to keep it safe from predators [Music]
